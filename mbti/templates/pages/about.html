About the framework

A clearer way to understand the 16 MBTI types

MBTI groups people by preferences in how they focus energy, gather information, make decisions, and relate to structure. This app keeps those comparisons simple, readable, and easy to revisit.

What the letters describe

Thinking vs. Feeling

How you make decisions.

Judging vs. Perceiving

How you approach structure and planning.

The science behind MBTI

MBTI is based on Carl Jung's theory of psychological types. It was developed by Isabel Briggs Myers and her mother, Katharine Cook Briggs, during the 1940s to make those ideas easier to understand and use in everyday life.

The framework is popular for self-reflection, career conversations, and relationship language, while still drawing criticism for simplifying people into categories. This app keeps the presentation straightforward so users can weigh the ideas for themselves.
